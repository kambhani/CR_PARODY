ARROWS

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Arrows, for simply 3 elixir, are far too powerful. The most prominent feature of the card is its staggered damage, which allows it to take out troops (like Guards) that no other troop can. Its damage takes out troops like Firecracker, Archers, and Minions. The radius of the card allows the user to be less precise with no drawbacks. For example, identifying tricky Goblin Barrels with Arrows is much simpler â€” just aim at the tower and most barrels are gone. Overall, Arrows offers too much value for the cost. The best way to make Arrows less OP is to reduce the radius by 0.5 tiles. This will make the card more high-skill while still retaining all interactions.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

Arrows suffers from problems that make it less useful than other spells. For one, its travel time makes aiming slightly more difficult, especially against Firecracker, one of the hardest cards to hit. The damage is also quite sad, being unable to even kill a Bomber. There is not too much wrong with it, but some buffs are in order. For one, a faster travel time (or even no travel time like Zap or Poison) is in order. A damage buff could also be employed, potentially up to 400 damage (so Bomber dies to the Spell). These changes could make the card more viable.
