EARTHQUAKE

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Earthquake is extremely overpowered for its measly cost of three elixir. For starters, the damage it does to buildings cripples their use on defense. A great example of this would be split Royal Hogs + Earthquake. Normally, the best way to counter this is to use a building, bring the hogs to the middle, and take out the distracted hogs with support troops. However, the Earthquake makes this impossible. The presence of the card in a deck also cripples X-Bow decks as their main win condition is a building. Another annoying aspect of the card is its damage as it is able to one-tick skeletons and take out Goblins in three ticks. This makes defending a Hog + Earthquake push with Skeleton Army or Goblin Gang a surefire way to lose the match. Additionally, the card does not do reduced tower damage, meaning that the card does more damage to the crown tower than a Fireball. This is simply unfair, and makes Earthquake cycling a reliable way to take off the last 500 to 600 damage on a tower. Finally, the slowdown effect (similar, but less potent than the Ram Rider's snare), makes defending pushes with the card that much harder. Overall, the card needs drastic nerfs to make it more fair. The best nerfs to give this card would be a crown tower damage reduction, a movement slowdown reduction, and an overall damage reduction.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

Earthquake is suffering from low use rates because of multiple inherent issues with the card. The main such one is that the card does not even do enough damage to kill a Princess or Dart Goblin. This makes users of the card suffer tremendously against Log Bait decks. Considering the prevalance of the bait archetype in general, Earthquake's pathetic damage needs fixing. Additionally, the card is rather weak in actually destroying buildings. A Hog + Earthquake push cannot even get past an Inferno Tower. These issues hold the card back, and they need to be addressed before the card is viable. The issues should be addressed by: increasing building damage to 3.5x normal damage, increase normal damage to 220, and (if really needed) increase the radius to four tiles.
