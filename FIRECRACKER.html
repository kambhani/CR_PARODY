FIRECRACKER

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Firecracker gives too much value for just three elixir. As a ground & air targetting splasher, its versatility is astounding. It makes short work of swarms and is useful on defense as it can attack the tank and still hit the support troops behind it, similar to a Magic Archer. The range of six tiles, more than that of Archers, is simply unfair. However, arguably the most annoying mechanic this card possesses is the pushback mechanic. Aiming spells at her is extremely difficult if you don't have Arrows. The card also give melee troops a hard time. Cards like Knight, Valkyrie, etc. are forced to walk large distances to even interact with the card. When placed right, this card can even kite melee troops which is just insane. Even troops with projectiles suffer, most notably the Bowler, whose slow attack speed coupled with the Firecracker's pushback results in a nightmare for the former. Overall, this card needs a nerf. The two most vital nerfs are an HP reduction to 216 (to make it loggable), and a pushback reduction of 0.5 tiles. These changes should make the card easier to handle.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

For three elixir, the card is simply quite underwhelming. For one, playing against the card means a free King Tower activation. For some decks, most notably Graveyard, this basically means a lost game. For this reason, Graveyard decks (as well as other decks like Goblin Barrel ones) simply cannot use the card. Furthermore, the card is hard to control due to its pushback effect. Often, though it is deployed in one lane, it will wander off into the other due to the pushback it gets. This is terribly annoying. In sum, there are changes that could be made to address the card's flaw. One such change would be to increase the range to seven tiles. This would stop the card from activating as many King Towers. Another important way to buff the card is to make it so that a naked Firecracker does not activate the King Tower in 2v2. These changes would make the card more viable.
