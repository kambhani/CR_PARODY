MINER

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Miner is the most used win condition in the game. It is one of the most broken cards in the game, if not the most broken. The OP nature of the card stems from its unique status as an instant tank. For just three elixir, the card can turn any defense into a solid counterpush that requires immediate addressing. The card's HP makes its role that much better, and by extension, that much harder to deal with. Pushes with the card can destroy a tower for as little as five elixir (Miner + Bats). Another annoying aspect about the card is that it can do hundreds of chip damage when left alone. This seems unfair â€” the Miner gets to be an instant tank AND a chip damage card? A further aspect of the card which is rather annoying is its range. This makes the card (when locked onto the crown tower) very difficult, if not impossible, to retarget by surrounding it with swarms. Miner is also one of the few win conditions that can acutally be useful on defense. Also, the Miner can actually tank both princess towers, allowing for some serious split-lane pressure. This card's versatility enables it to be in nearly any deck. From Mega Knight Bait to Lava Miner to Miner cycle to Miner Balloon, this card is seen everywhere. Overall, this card could use some drastic nerfs to make it more fair. These include: a crown tower damage multiplier of 0.1 instead of the current 0.35 (for less chip damage), an HP reduction by 5% (for less tanking), and a range reduction to Melee: Short (for easier retargetting). These changes should make the card less oppressive to face.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

Miner is a decent card but it lacks some stats that could really make the card great. To start off, the card has a rather unfortunate amount of hitpoints, especially when compared to the Knight. It also has less DPS than this card and even less crown tower damage to boot. Its deploy time takes forever which makes it very hard to use on defense. Activating King Tower with this card is also way too easy. Many decks that could use this card opt for other cards instead because of its flaws. For example the KT activation potential means that nearly no Graveyard deck runs the card. Its overall paltry defensive capability explains why decks like Classic Log Bait opt for Knight instead. Overall, this card could use buffs to make it better. Such buffs could include an HP buff, a faster deploy time, and a greater mass (to make KT activations harder). These changes should help the card.
