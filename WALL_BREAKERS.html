WALL BREAKERS

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Wall Breakers offer far too much value for the 2 elixir cost it possesses. As the cheapest win condition in the game, the card is used to constantly pressure the opponent. Because of this, the card is found chiefly in cycle decks, particularly those that have the Miner. The HP of the card also allows it to be split in the middle for a total commitment of one elixir per lane. Left unattended, this simple action can deal over 300 damage in each lane. Countering split-lane Wall Breakers often involves a negative elixir trade. As if this were not enough, the card can also be used to kite troops like the Bandit, adding defensive value to a primarily offensive tool. Overall, this card needs nerfs to make it more balance. The best one would be to nerf health so split-lane Wall Breakers is not as big of a threat. A damage reduction can also be explored if the HP nerf is not enough.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

Wall Breakers need buffs to make them more viable, because in their current state, they simply cannot be used. The main reason for this is that the card possesses virtually no value if the deck does not also contain Miner. This vastly limits the amount of decks the card can be in. Hence, the best way to make Wall Breakers more prominent is to make it more viable without Miner. Some ways to do this include: a HP buff so Miner does not need to tank for Wall Breakers most of the time, a damage buff because the card rarely makes it to the tower (so when it does, the user should be rewarded more), and a splash radius increase (to better damage opposing troops). These changes will make the card more versatile, thus increasing use rates.
