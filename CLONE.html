CLONE

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Clone is overpowered and needs drastic nerfs to fix its problems. For starters, it basically doubles the DPS of a push. This makes the card very deadly, especially when paired with high DPS cards like Balloon and Skeleton Barrel. In fact, there are entire decks that revolve around abusing the power this card provides. The most prominent example of this is Lava Clone. By using a Clone and hitting multiple air targets like Lava Hound, Baby Dragon, and Bats, the already stressed aerial defense of the opponent are pushed past breaking point. Another deck that has appeared more recently into the meta is a Golem bait deck, comprised of the following: Golem, Night Witch, Flying Machine, Clone, Goblin Gang, Bats, Skeleton Barrel, and Arrows. These toxic decks are made possible by the card known as Clone. A great synergy with Clone is Freeze. Simply Freeze all defense and Clone for an easy tower. Clearly, this card needs a nerf. The best way to do this is to either reduce the radius or set a limit on how many troops can be cloned. But in all honesty, any sort of nerf should put the broken spell in its rightful place.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

Clone is an underused card and the reasons for this should be obvious. First and foremost, all of the splash in the meta has made it difficult to use the card effectively. Even decks that lack splash units can use spells to rid the field of cloned troops. The prevalance of splash is just one reason that decks like Lava Clone have fallen into obscurity. It also explains why Clone is one of the least used cards in the game. Another reason that Clone is underpowered is that its radius is too small. Often, separating troops in a push is vital to avoid giving the opponent spell. However, this comes with the cost of not being able to clone the entire push. Finally, since Clone does not do any damage, it often sits unused in a player's hand. Overall, Clone could use buffs to bring it back into the spotlight. Good changes to advance this goal include a radius increase and the addition of damage to the card, similar to Freeze. These changes will help diversify the meta and thereby make it better.
