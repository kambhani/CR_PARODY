GOBLINS

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Goblins is a very good card and in desparate need of nerfs. For one, its cheap cost enables cycling. In fact, the card can inflict tower damage when placed at the bridge and the opponent ignore it. Also, their high hitpoints enable the card to survive a zap or snowball, making the card very annoying to get rid of. Goblins can deal an astonishing 270 DPS at tournament standard, making short work of troops like Bandit, Sparky, and Mini Pekka (with the help of the princess tower). In nearly every way, Goblins are beefed-up skeletons, and seeing how that card is seen as OP, it leaves little doubt as to the OP nature of the card at hand. To make Goblins less of a threat, the number of goblins should be reduced to 2. Changing goblin count is the only way to rework Goblins without affecting the other card which use it. The day the count is reduced to two is most definitely the day in which Clash Royale rises back into glory.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

Goblins suffer from below-average use rates in both ladder and grand challenges, and currently, the card is inferior to Skeletons, Spear Goblins, and Goblin Gang, all of which have higher use rates. Skeletons, though inferior to Goblins in nearly every aspect, are cheaper, and thus fit into more decks. Spear Goblins, though they have lower HP, can attack air, making it a good card in Miner cycle decks. Finally, Goblin Gang offer significantly more value for one more elixir. Goblins needs buffs to be useful, and the best one would be to have four goblins and not three. This would make the card an actual alternative to the other cards I mentioned above. The meta would also benefit from having Goblins in it.
