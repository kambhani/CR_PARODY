PRINCESS

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Princess is way too broken for the game. The concept of a troop that could outrange a tower is problematic. However, this issue can be resolved by making the card very weak to balance the unique property. However, Princess is not very weak; she is in fact a strong card that sees use in a myriad of decks. An analysis of what makes the card broken must first start with her range of nine tiles. This is very large and is in fact unnecessary, seeing that the Princess Towers only have a range of 7.5 tiles. The extra range the Princess possesses lets it get insane value on defense. It also makes bridge spamming the card a viable chip damage option as that is nearly unreactable. Another issue with the card is its HP. The card was supposed to be fragile, but it has a little too much HP. For example, it survives an Earthquake, giving many decks with the card a bad matchup against Princess. The card also survives a round of shots by the Skeletons. This means that if the Princess user has a spell ready, they can bridge spam the card, get chip damage on the tower, and reactively spell away the Skeletons. Finally, there is the issue of splash damage. The splash radius is quite wide, meaning that the card can make short work of swarms despite her hit speed. These factors make her quite annoying, especially when used in her primary archetype, Log Bait. She synergizes extremely well with Inferno Tower, taking out various distracting troops and allowing the building to target the tank. She is excellent spell bait and cannot be ignored. Overall, these features demand a nerf for the card. To effectively nerf the card, the following changes must be implemented: a range reduction by one tile, an HP nerf by 10%, and a slight splash radius nerf. These changes will stop her from ruining the meta.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

Princess is too weak in its current form. It is only viable in Log Bait decks, but even then, she struggles to get significant value. The Log and Barbarian Barrel will counter the card for a positive elixir trade. If one does not use these cards, then Arrows and Miner will counter the card for an even elixir trade. Nearly all decks run at least one of these Princess sniping cards. This makes it hard on Princess users. Additionally, the card shoots so slowly. It seems that the card is not effective at rapid clearing because of this. This makes her outclassed by other cards like Dart Goblin and Bats. Her DPS is rather sad and could use a slight buff. The most prominent competitor to the card is Firecracker. She has replaced the Princess in various Hog Rider decks. Buffs to the Princess should help remedy this issue. Some buffs (which she deserves) include a hitspeed reduction and an HP buff. These modifications should make her useful in non-bait decks.
