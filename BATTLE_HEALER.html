BATTLE HEALER

WHY IT IS OVERPOWERED

Battle Healer is a broken card that needs nerfs. As a staple in the no-skill Elixir Golem deck, it has proven to the CR world just how annoying it is. One of the problems with the card is that it has so many hitpoints, enough to survive a Rocket. This is inherently unfair, seeing as other cards like Executioner and Sparky (which cost more elixir) die to it. Spelling out the card is a horrible elixir trade, meaning that the only way to take out the card is through troops. With a tank in front of the card, however, that proves very challenging as the heal mechanic of the card comes into play (another broken aspect of the card). Whenever the card attacks an opposing troop, it heals everything around it (including itself). Destroying the card then becomes near impossible. Finally, there is the issue of the card's passive healing. This basically mitigates any spell value on the card. Overall, Battle Healer is a no-skill overpowered card that needs serious nerfs. The best nerfs would be to reduce HP so it dies to Rocket, prevent the card from active healing itself, and reduce (if not remove) passive healing. These changes should stop Battle Healer from being oppressive.

WHY IT IS UNDERPOWERED

Battle Healer is a weak card that needs a buff. The only card that it is viable with is Elixir Golem. This is an inherent burden on the card which prevents it from being versatile. Consequently, its use rates are very low. Battle Healer is basically a worse Knight / Valkyrie. Its DPS is also pathetic. Though a low DPS helps its offensive capabilites, it severely hampers its defensive capabilities. This hurts the versatility of the card. The healing the card produces is also weak and could use a buff. Finally, the HP of the card makes it die very quickly on defense. Overall, the card is weak and needs buffs. Buffs it needs include an HP buff, a healing buff, a DPS buff, and a Heal Spirit spawn upon death. These changes should make Battle Healer vesatile enough to fit in decks besides Elixir Golem ones.
